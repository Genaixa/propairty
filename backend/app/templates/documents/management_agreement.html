1. Appointment

The Landlord hereby appoints {{ org_name }} as sole managing agent for the property or properties listed above, and the Agent accepts such appointment on the terms and conditions set out in this Agreement.

This Agreement shall commence on {{ today }} and shall continue until terminated by either party giving not less than {{ notice_period }} days' written notice to the other party.

2. Services Provided

The Agent agrees to provide the following services on behalf of the Landlord:

  • Letting Service: Marketing the property, conducting viewings, referencing prospective tenants, preparing and executing Assured Shorthold Tenancy Agreements.
  • Rent Collection: Collecting rent on the due date and remitting net proceeds to the Landlord monthly, after deducting fees and authorised expenditure.
  • Property Management: Dealing with day-to-day management queries from tenants, arranging repairs and maintenance with pre-approved limits.
  • Inspections: Conducting periodic property inspections (minimum {{ inspection_frequency }}) and reporting findings to the Landlord.
  • Compliance: Monitoring statutory compliance requirements including Gas Safety, EICR, EPC, and Right to Rent checks, and advising the Landlord accordingly.
  • Deposit Management: Collecting and registering security deposits with a government-approved tenancy deposit scheme.
  • Legal Notices: Serving legally required notices (Section 21, Section 8, Section 13, etc.) as instructed by the Landlord.
  • Statements: Providing monthly income and expenditure statements.

4. Maintenance Authority

The Agent is authorised to arrange and approve emergency and routine repairs up to £{{ maintenance_limit }} per instruction without seeking the Landlord's prior approval. Works exceeding this limit will be submitted to the Landlord for approval before being instructed, except in genuine emergency where safety or habitability is at risk.

All contractor costs shall be deducted from rental income or invoiced separately as agreed.

5. Landlord's Obligations

The Landlord agrees to:

  • Ensure the property is fit for habitation and complies with all relevant legislation at the commencement of any tenancy;
  • Hold valid buildings and contents insurance, and landlord liability insurance;
  • Maintain a valid Gas Safety Certificate (where applicable), EICR, and EPC in date;
  • Provide the Agent with sufficient funds to cover expenditure where rental income is insufficient;
  • Notify the Agent of any change of ownership, mortgage, or legal charge over the property;
  • Comply with the Landlord and Tenant Act 1985, Housing Act 1988, and all other applicable legislation;
  • Ensure any mortgage lender's consent to let is in place where required.

6. Agent's Obligations

The Agent agrees to:

  • Act in the best interests of the Landlord at all times;
  • Hold client money in a designated client account, separate from the Agent's own funds;
  • Be a member of a recognised Client Money Protection scheme;
  • Be a member of a Property Redress Scheme;
  • Maintain professional indemnity insurance;
  • Comply with the provisions of the Tenant Fees Act 2019 and all other applicable legislation.

7. Termination

Either party may terminate this Agreement by giving not less than {{ notice_period }} days' written notice. On termination, the Agent shall provide the Landlord with all relevant documentation, keys, deposit scheme details, and outstanding rental funds within 14 days.

The Landlord shall remain liable for the Agent's fees in respect of any tenancy arranged by the Agent that is still subsisting at the date of termination.

8. Data Protection

Both parties agree to comply with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018. The Agent will process personal data of the Landlord and tenants only for the purposes of providing the services under this Agreement.

9. Governing Law

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the law of England and Wales, and both parties submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of England and Wales.
